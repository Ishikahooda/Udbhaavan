Downstream

Industries form the backbone of a country. It generates employment and provides the people associated with it the means to earn their daily bread and a country the revenue for itself. One such industry is chemical industries. These are places where chemicals, fuels, fossils, natural gas, plastics, food, paint; fertilisers are processed, refined, stored and transferred. These chemicals find applications in laboratories, research, fuels for machinery, fuels for car, daily use products, cleaning liquids, fertilisers, fungicides, herbicides, cement, paints, cosmetics and much more.
These chemicals are not only highly useful, but also hazardous for humans. And some of them at the time of production exist in gaseous state. Just like air, the chemicals in this state are colourless and odourless, giving them an invisibility cloak. In industries all across the world these chemicals are stored and transferred in sealed and secured vessels and pipelines. But what happens when the safety of a vessel is not guaranteed? What happens when the pipeline starts to leak?
Leakage of natural gas pipelines may cause explosions and fires, resulting in casualties, environmental damage, and material loss. Meanwhile, the leakage of hazardous chemicals and their ingestion by humans can lead to instant deaths.
The fate of the city lies in your hands. Can you stop the spread of an invisible, deadly monster which can turn your living city into morgue?

Rules and regulations:
The Event is divided in two rounds.
In the first round, participants will be provided a case study following which they will answer/discuss the questionnaire. The timing for Round 1 will be 30 mins.
In the second round, the teams will be faced with Gas Leakage simulation, and their task will be to fix the leakage and minimise the damage. Each team will be provided 5 mins to work on the simulation. Read through the case study and prepare a document on ways to detect, avoid the gas leakages in a pipeline.
Only ONE team from each school will be allowed to participate.
The entry is open for 6,7,8 and 9 class.
A team can have not more than 4 members.
The participation will be based on first-cum-first-serve basis.

Judgement Criteria:
Effectiveness of Solution, Feasibility of solution, Presentation

Arena Details:
The arena will consist of simulation of pipeline distribution network with a supply line.

Space Bar

Background
Can we ever build our dream house on moon? What if we could design a space of our dreams anywhere in space? These free-space settlements could be wonderful places to live; endowed with weightless recreation, fantastic views, freedom, and great wealth.
The NASA’S Space Settlement Design Competition puts school students in the shoes of aerospace industry engineers designing a city in space that will be a home for over 10,000 people. Student engineers demonstrate creativity, technical competence, management skills, space environment knowledge, teamwork, and presentation techniques to conquer the problems inherent in designing a Space Settlement (aka Space Colony).

Event Guidelines:
Students will create and design representative models/ presentation of space settlement colonies, which can be habitable for people. Students can follow NASA webpage, which has some proposed settlement structures and designs to help them in building a model.

Objective:
To create innovative design for outer space colonies.

Type:
Competitive

Materials Required:
Students are required to make a model or a design and bring it during Udbhaavan and present their Idea to the judges. They are free to use whatever material they want to.

Key Skills required:
  • • Students should be able to analyze a given situation and data provided.
  • • Students should be able to think critically about a problem.
  • • Students should be able to think creatively and create a model or a design.
  • • Students should be able to pitch in their project idea.


Judgment Criteria:
The Judgment will be based on the following Criteria:
  • A. Sustainability of the model.
  • B. Clear Rationale behind the design.
  • C. Functionality of the design


How to Prepare:
  • • Ask students to go through and Explore NASA’s International Space Settlement Competition and see the student’s design and Projects for creating outer space colonies.
  • • Ask students to create their own designs, model and along with that they can also prepare a presentation to pitch their ideas.
    • Push them to think how their model will be sustainable and economical. Push for the following 3 things:
    • A. The Structural design (Is it sustainable?)
    • B. Operation (How much time would it take to build the actual design and how would you send that to space?)
    • C. Material used for preparing that structure (Are they Economic or not?)
    • D. Human Factor (How many people will stay in that structure? How will they stay there? Will there be facilities to meet the basic human needs- food, oxygen, energy, clean water, medical kits etc.)
