Junkyard Wars

This event aims to develop Critical Thinking, Creativity, Innovation and Problem Solving in kids. It aims to appraise how kids will be able to use these skills and create something out of scrap together as a Team.

Event Guidelines:
Participants will be provided with case studies for reference and some materials they have to brainstorm for all the possible designs they can create out of it. Participants are free to drive their imagination and think of all the possible designs and build them. Extra credit points will be given to the working models, the models designed for tackling environmental issues like pollution etc.

Rules and Regulations:
1. 10 Ideas are given for reference on event day. 4 of them will be picked. 2. The team should consist of 2-4 members. 3. Students will be given 1 hour to prepare their designs.

Judgement Criteria:
Judges will evaluate each model. Extra Credit points will be given to working model. Judgment criteria for each case are given in the section case for reference.

Case Studies:
  • 1. Crossbow:
    Using Ice Cream Sticks, Sticks, Pencils, Rubber Bands etc make a crossbow the design made should be strong and apply the required pressure on the bow that it go furthest.
  • 2. Vacuum Cleaner:
    Using Plastic Bottle, Motor and tin sheet create a Vaccum Cleaner.
  • 3. Weight support structure:
    Using straws, cardboards, paper clips, rubber bands, plastics, cloths clips, ice-cream sticks and other used materials to make structure which can support maximum weight possible. The structure will be analysed on various aspects of physics laws and understanding of geometrical configurations.
  • 4. Steamboat:
    Using straws, cardboards, paper clips, rubber bands, plastics, cloths clips, ice- cream sticks, foil, disposable cups and other used materials to make stem boat sustaining maximum weight possible.
  • 5. Tower:
    Using materials such as newspaper, scissors, tape, ice-cream sticks, paper clips, straws etc. create the tallest freestanding tower.
  • 6. Parachute Drop:
    Using straws, bags, rubber bands, scissors, etc. design a parachute that will have the lowest drop and earn points for accuracy, too.
  • 7. Create a catapult:
    Using ice-cream sticks, rubber band, straws, papers, cups, tubes and all possible available junk material create a catapult. During the competition students used pennies or stones earned points for distance and accuracy.
  • 8. Rubber Band Car:
    Use above mentioned junk materials and used plastic bottle making car using physics of kinetic energy.
  • 9. Hydraulic Crane:
    Using cardboard, battery, wire and Junk Material provided make a Hydraulic Crane. The judgment will be based on (A) Working Model (B) Lifting Maximum Weight.
  • 10. Free-to-do:
    Use any material on the site and create anything. The Judgment will be based on the How creative designs are? The rationale behind making the design? Is it solving an Environmental Issue which you strongly feel about? Is it a working model?
